# Content discipline — write something that means something

Pronunciation-perfect lyrics with rubbish content are still rubbish. This file is the field guide for keeping the content meaningful while every other quality control runs.

A lyric passes content discipline when a listener can answer four questions after hearing it:

1. **What is this song about?** (one specific sentence)
2. **What does the speaker feel, and why?** (one word + a because)
3. **Who is the speaker talking to or about?** (a specific person, not "you" in general)
4. **What changes between the start and the end?** (the arc)

If you can't answer any of these from your own draft, your draft has a content problem.

---

## The four locks (set these before drafting)

### Lock 1 — One emotional axis

Pick **one** of:

- longing
- regret
- defiance
- peace
- hope
- grief
- anger
- gratitude
- doubt
- acceptance

Don't try to be sad **and** celebratory in the same 4 verses. A song that's "happy in the chorus, sad in the verse" is fine — that's contrast on one axis (joy/sorrow). A song that's "lonely + angry + hopeful + nostalgic" is incoherent.

### Lock 2 — One concrete situation

A theme is not a situation. A situation is a moment, an action, a tangible thing.

| Theme (avoid as starting point) | Situation (use this)                                    |
|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------|
| love                              | I left my key on her table and didn't take mine back   |
| loneliness                        | the cup of tea went cold while I was waiting           |
| nostalgia                         | the smell of my mother's tea reaches me through years  |
| betrayal                          | I read your text from someone else over your shoulder  |
| hope                              | I bought a second cup, in case you come                |

Write the situation in one sentence. Build the lyric around it.

### Lock 3 — One narrative arc

What changes from verse 1 to the final chorus? Pick one:

- **Discovery** — speaker learns something they didn't know
- **Departure** — speaker leaves (literally or emotionally)
- **Return** — speaker comes back (or asks the other to)
- **Refusal** — speaker decides not to do/feel something
- **Acceptance** — speaker stops fighting the situation
- **Escalation** — feeling intensifies
- **Deflation** — feeling collapses or releases

Write the arc in one sentence: *"Verse 1: she's gone. Verse 2: I'm starting to admit she's not coming back. Chorus: I leave the light on anyway. Bridge: I realise the light is for me, not her. Final chorus: I leave it on."*

### Lock 4 — One specific perspective

Pin down:

- **Speaker**: first person? collective "we"? observer?
- **Addressee**: who is "تو"? The lover? The city? A past self? A friend? Pick one and hold it.
- **Time of speaking**: now? looking back? mid-action?
- **Setting**: where is the speaker as they sing this? home? on a road? in a memory?

Settings can shift, but only with a marked turn (a `[Bridge]` or a clear time word). Random shifts confuse the listener.

## While drafting

### Replace abstract nouns with concrete objects

| Abstract (vague)        | Concrete (specific)                       |
|--------------------------|-------------------------------------------|
| دلتنگی                  | صندلیِ خالیِ تو                         |
| خاطره                   | بوی پیراهنت روی کوسن                     |
| تنهایی                  | فنجونِ دومی که هیچوقت پر نشد            |
| فاصله                   | فاصله‌ی یه ایستگاهِ مترو                |
| سکوت                    | صدای یخچال که حالا میشنوم                |
| سرما                    | پتوی روی مبل که هنوز جای پات هست        |

The more specific, the more emotionally true. A listener feels a teacup; they don't feel "نوستالژی".

### Ban these placeholder words

In a lyric, these are signs the writer didn't bother:

- همه چی / همه چیز
- هیچی / هیچ چیز
- یه جوری / یه چیزی
- همه جا
- یه روزی (without a specific follow-up)
- اون / این (without antecedent)

Replace each with a specific noun, action, or moment. If you can't replace, the line itself probably isn't needed.

### Show actions, don't name emotions

Naming an emotion is telling. Showing an action that *implies* the emotion is singing.

| Telling (avoid)           | Showing (use)                                |
|---------------------------|----------------------------------------------|
| گریه کردم                | لیوان از دستم افتاد                          |
| دوستت دارم                | هنوز پنجره رو به سمت تو باز می‌ذارم        |
| دلم تنگ شده               | شب‌ها برای دو نفر چای میذارم                 |
| خوشحال شدم                | کلید رو دو بار توی قفل چرخوندم               |
| ترسیدم                    | نفسم رو نگه داشتم تا ماشین رد بشه           |
| رفت                       | کفش‌هاش دیگه دمِ در نبودن                    |

One showing > five tellings.

### Pick one metaphor system per song

If the song is **water-themed** (دریا, موج, شنا, غرق), don't suddenly add **fire** (شعله, آتش, سوختن) — unless the contrast is the *point* and you mark it explicitly.

Common metaphor systems:

- water / sea / drowning / waves
- fire / burning / ash / smoke
- night / dark / shadow / dawn
- road / journey / arrival / departure
- light / lamp / window / candle
- weather / rain / cold / storm
- house / room / door / chair / bed
- body / hand / heart / eyes / breath

Pick one as the spine. A secondary system is OK if it serves the spine.

### Pronoun continuity

Track «من», «تو», «ما», «اون». They must be the same entities across the song.

- If verse 1's «تو» is your lover, verse 2's «تو» can't be your past self without a marked turn.
- If verse 1's «اون» is the rain, verse 2's «اون» can't be a person without re-introducing them.

The most common AI failure: a song where «تو» means three different people across four verses.

### Time continuity

Pick one structure:

- **All present**: every verse is happening now.
- **Past → Present**: verses 1–2 in the past, chorus/bridge in the present.
- **Present → Future**: verses describe now, bridge/final-chorus reach forward.
- **Flashback**: chorus is now, verses are then.

Random tense shifts inside a single verse are nonsense. If the speaker is in a memory and steps out of it, the step out should be obvious («حالا که اینجام»).

## The seven nonsense patterns (and the fix)

### 1. Empty rhyme chain

```
درد
سرد
مرد
فرد
```

Words rhyme; no narrative connects them. **Fix**: take any pair, build a specific situation around them, drop the others.

### 2. Mixed metaphor

```
چشمِ ماهْ درِ سکوتِ قلبم رو باز کرد
```

Three systems (eye, moon, door, silence, heart). The listener can't picture this. **Fix**: pick one — *"the moon opened my window"* or *"my heart's door is silent tonight"*.

### 3. Generic palette

```
شب تاریک، بارون می‌باره
قلبم تنها، تنها میسوزه
```

These words can build any song or no song. **Fix**: replace at least two words with specifics tied to the locked situation. *"چراغ خونه روشن مونده، چای من هنوز بخار نکرده"*.

### 4. Cliché stack

```
شبِ تاریک، قلبِ شکسته، اشک‌های بی‌رحم
```

Each phrase is already someone else's song title. The sum is wallpaper. **Fix**: find the *one* original image you actually have and let it carry the line.

### 5. Contradiction without a turn

```
[Verse 1]
هیچوقت فراموش نمی‌کنم
دارم فراموش می‌کنم
```

Both lines might be true, but they can't sit next to each other without a marked emotional turn. **Fix**: put the contradiction across `[Verse]` → `[Bridge]`, with the bridge naming the change.

### 6. Subject–predicate mismatch

```
صدای دستِ بادِ خاطره
```

Each word is fine; the chain says nothing. **Fix**: ask "*what is doing what to what*?" — if you can't answer, the line is grammar without meaning. Rewrite as a sentence first, then poeticise.

### 7. Filler verse

```
[Verse 2]
شب رفت، صبح اومد
دلم گرفت، آسمون باخت
هر چی بود، تموم شد
```

This verse adds nothing to the song. It rhymes, it scans, it means nothing. **Fix**: cut it, or replace it with a verse that *progresses* the arc — show the speaker discovering, deciding, or doing something.

## The paraphrase test (run before delivery)

In one sentence, paraphrase the song. Examples of passing paraphrases:

- *"The speaker keeps a chair empty for someone who isn't coming back, and the chorus is them admitting it."*
- *"The speaker is on a night drive thinking about whether to send a message they shouldn't send; the bridge is them deciding not to."*
- *"The speaker is making coffee in their old kitchen the morning after a death."*

Examples of failing paraphrases (rewrite required):

- *"It's about love and loneliness."* — too vague; could fit a thousand songs.
- *"It's about how everything is gone but there's still hope."* — clichés in series, no situation.
- *"It's about a person."* — no, it isn't.

If your paraphrase fails, the lyric fails. Rewrite the verses until the paraphrase passes.

---

## The "cut by half" rule

After your first draft, **cut any line that you can't defend** with one of these:

- It progresses the arc.
- It shows the situation specifically.
- It carries the metaphor system forward.
- It earns its rhyme by saying something the rhyme couldn't have predicted.

A song with 4 great lines and 0 filler beats a song with 16 lines where 12 are decoration.

## Persian-specific cliché list (use sparingly or not at all)

These appear in 80% of Persian song drafts. Each is technically correct; collectively they're wallpaper:

- شبِ تاریک / شبِ تنها
- قلبِ شکسته
- اشک‌های بی‌رحم
- بارانِ بی‌امان
- جاده‌ی بی‌انتها
- خورشیدِ غمگین
- خاطره‌ی تو
- دردِ دلِ من
- آسمونِ گریون
- ستاره‌ی بی‌سو
- پنجره‌ی بسته
- ساعتِ بی‌رحم

If you must use one, pair it with one specific image that anchors it to *this* song. *"بارانِ بی‌امان"* on its own is wallpaper; *"بارانِ بی‌امان روی ماشینِ تو که هنوز جلوی خونه‌ست"* is a song.

## Final test: read it to yourself as a person, not a singer

Before delivering, read the lyric in your head as if a friend wrote it and sent it to you. Two questions:

1. **Did anything happen?** (Did the speaker discover, decide, leave, return, refuse, accept, or change?)
2. **Could anyone else have written this *exact* song?** (If the answer is "any Persian-speaking adult", the content failed.)

If either fails, rewrite. Pronunciation-perfect rubbish is still rubbish.
