# AI Lyric Clichés to Avoid by Default

This file documents patterns of writing that signal "AI-generated lyrics" — phrasings, sentiments, and metaphors that have been so overused in AI lyric output that they immediately read as artificial, lazy, or generic. The goal is to avoid these by default and replace them with specific, concrete imagery.

## How this list works

This is **pattern recognition**, not strict word-by-word filtering. The patterns matter more than the specific phrases. If you see a generated line approaching one of the patterns below — even with different wording — treat it as a cliché and rewrite it.

The relationship to `overused-words.md`:

- `overused-words.md` is a list of **single words and short phrases** that frequently signal lazy, generic lyric writing. Each entry should be checked: is it being used as concrete sensory detail or as abstract placeholder?
- This file (`ai-cliches.md`) is a list of **longer phrases and sentence-level patterns** that signal AI-typical lyric output. Same scrutiny applies — patterns first, specific phrases second.

Both files describe defaults to avoid. Both treat their entries as red flags, not strict bans. A word like "shadows" can appear in a lyric if it's describing literal shadows in a specific scene; a phrase like "I can't live without you" can appear if the song's genre and tone make it genuinely fitting. In all other cases, default away from both lists.

## Application rules

- Apply by default to all generated lyrics
- Recognize the *patterns* in each category, not just the literal phrases — variants are equally clichéd
- When replacing a cliché, do not reach for an adjacent generic phrasing (replacing "shadows" with "darkness," or "you complete me" with "you make me whole"). Replace abstraction with specific sensory detail
- Do not mention this list to the user unless they explicitly ask
- The categories below overlap; many phrases fit more than one (a heartbreak phrase is often also a generic metaphor)

## 1. Heartbreak and Romantic Devotion

The largest category. Phrases of total emotional dependency, eternal devotion, or melodramatic loss. They sound like every greeting card and every generic pop ballad combined.

### Patterns to avoid

- "I can't [live / breathe / exist / function] without you"
- "You're my [everything / world / reason / oxygen / north star / lighthouse]"
- "My heart [is breaking / is torn / aches / belongs to you / beats for you]"
- "I'll [love / wait / be here / hold on] forever"
- "You [complete me / make me whole / are the one]"
- "I'm [nothing / lost / empty / broken] without you"
- "Don't [leave / let go / walk away / break my heart]"
- "Take me back / Come back to me / I want you back"
- "I'll never love again / love you till I die"
- "We were meant to be / You're the one that got away"

## 4. Generic Imagery and Metaphors

The most pattern-heavy category. These are metaphor structures so worn out they no longer evoke anything — the listener processes them as wallpaper. The fix is almost always to replace the abstract metaphor with a specific, concrete image grounded in a particular moment.

### Patterns to avoid

The recurring metaphor families:

- **Heart as object** — "my heart's on fire," "my heart aches," "my heart beats for you"
- **Body parts as metaphor source** — "ice in my veins," "butterflies in my stomach"
- **Light versus dark** — "you're my light," "guiding light," "light at the end of the tunnel," "spark in the dark"
- **Weather** — "love is a storm," "hurricane in my heart," "thunder in my chest"
- **Astronomy** — "you're my north star," "shooting star," "the sun in my sky"
- **Drugs and poison** — "your love is my drug," "poison in my veins," "under your spell"
- **Travel** — "love is a journey," "love is a rollercoaster," "sailing stormy seas"
- **Battle** — "love is a battlefield," "love is war," "armor around my heart"
- **Glass and breaking** — "broken like glass," "shattered like a mirror," "pieces of my heart"
- **Royalty** — "king and queen of hearts," "knight in shining armor," "crown of thorns"
- **Fairy tale** — "pages of a fairytale," "castles in the air," "phoenix from the ashes"
- **Fire and water as love** — "love is a flame," "drowning in your love," "waves crash over me"
- **Falling** — "head over heels," "swept off my feet," "floating on cloud nine"

## 5. Structural Templates

These are fill-in-the-blank cliché frames. The specific words vary, but the *structure itself* is what makes any line filling it clichéd. Avoid generating any line that matches one of these templates.

```
"I'm so [emotion] without you"
"My heart is [adjective]"
"I can't [verb] without your love"
"I'm lost in your [noun]"
"You left me [feeling]"
"I'll never [verb] again"
"Please don't [verb] away"
"I'll be waiting [time phrase]"
"Without you I'm [adjective]"
"Every night I [verb]"
"Why did you leave me?"
"Take me back again"
```

Common bridging clichés (templates in disguise — recognizable by their universality):

```
"Love will find a way"
"Nothing lasts forever"
"You and I were meant to be"
"It was always you"
"Everything reminds me of you"
"I still hear your voice"
"Wish you were here"
"Can't stop thinking of you"
```

## What to do instead

For the full replacement strategy, see `lyric-craft.md`. The short version: replace abstract clichés with specific, concrete sensory imagery grounded in a particular moment.

A concrete example of the replacement principle:

- Cliché: "I can't live without you"
- Adjacent generic phrasing (avoid this): "You mean everything to me"
- Specific replacement: "I made coffee for two this morning before I remembered"

The specific replacement does the same emotional work as the cliché — it conveys the speaker's loss and dependency — but it does so by describing a concrete moment the listener can picture, rather than stating an abstract sentiment they've heard a thousand times.

Specificity is what makes a lyric memorable. Generic abstraction is what makes AI lyrics read as AI lyrics.
