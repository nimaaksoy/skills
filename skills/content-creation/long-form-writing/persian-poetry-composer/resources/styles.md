# Classical Persian style reference

One card per voice. Each card lists the signature moves, common forms, common vazn (meter), voice, and the failure modes to avoid.

---

## سعدی (Saadi) — fluent simplicity

- **Signature**: سهل ممتنع — looks easy, is not. Syntactic music. Restrained imagery. One small surprise per beyt.
- **Forms**: غزل، قصیده، مثنوی (Bustan, Golestan).
- **Common vazn**: highly varied; رمل مثمن مخبون محذوف is a safe default.
- **Voice**: warm, conversational, ethically grounded, never performative.
- **Signature moves**:
  - Syntactic deletion that creates emotional gap (e.g. "من در میان جمع و دلم جای دیگر است").
  - Subverted expectation in the second مصراع.
  - Soft tamsil (extended simile) in 1–2 beyts.
  - Direct second-person address.
- **Vocabulary leans on**: عشق، یار، دل، صبر، ادب، گلستان، باد، گل.
- **Don't**: heavy compound words, archaic verbs for archaism's sake, dense allusions, mystical opacity.
- **Common failure**: flat simplicity that mistakes "easy" for "shallow". Saadi's surface ease conceals careful syntactic engineering.

## حافظ (Hafez) — engineered ambiguity

- **Signature**: دو-پهلویی — multi-meaning, controlled. Ihaam (ایهام), iham-tanasob (ایهام تناسب), and tasel-mihaad (تلمیح) layered around a recurring word.
- **Forms**: غزل almost exclusively.
- **Common vazn**: رمل مثمن مخبون محذوف، مجتث مثمن مخبون محذوف، مضارع مثمن اخرب مکفوف محذوف.
- **Voice**: rind (رند) — the unreliable narrator; both pious and sceptical, both lover and critic.
- **Signature moves**:
  - Pick a دو-پهلو word (آینه، جام، خرابات، صبح، زلف، رند) and write each beyt to fire two interpretations.
  - Allusion (تلمیح) to Quranic phrasing, mystical lore, or court politics, often slanted.
  - Suspended syntax — sentence completes only at the end of the beyt, sometimes the غزل.
  - Network effect: the same word means three things across three different beyts in one غزل.
- **Vocabulary leans on**: جام، می، آینه، صبح، زلف، خرابات، رند، شیخ، پیر، مغان.
- **Don't**: use ambiguity that has no specific second meaning. "Murky" is not Hafez; *engineered* multi-meaning is.
- **Common failure**: faux-mystical fog. Before writing each beyt, write down the *two* specific readings you intend.

## مولانا (Rumi) — energy and motion

- **Signature**: حرکت + ندا + تکرار — verbs in imperative, repeated calls, dynamic imagery. The poem moves; it doesn't sit.
- **Forms**: مثنوی (Masnavi-ye Ma'navi), غزل (Divan-e Shams).
- **Common vazn**:
  - مثنوی: فاعلاتن فاعلاتن فاعلن.
  - غزل: many; the Divan is organised by vazn, suggesting any of them could be the right fit.
- **Voice**: ecstatic, urgent, sometimes oracular, sometimes intimate.
- **Signature moves**:
  - Open with a verb: بیا، برخیز، بزن، بنوش، بشکن.
  - Repetition of a single word for rhythmic and emotional intensification (each repetition shifts meaning or intensity).
  - Imagery that moves: dance, fire, river, road, breath, wing.
  - Paradox as resolution: "رفتن خود بازگشت است".
  - Question-answer micro-dialogue inside a beyt.
- **Vocabulary leans on**: نی، رقص، آتش، راه، دریا، شمس، عشق، جان، خویش، بی‌خویش.
- **Don't**: static description, ornamental adjectives, mysticism without bodily motion.
- **Common failure**: shouting without progression. Each repetition must shift intensity or direction; otherwise it's noise.

## فردوسی (Ferdowsi) — narrative steel

- **Signature**: روایت در وزن استوار — narrative in unwavering meter. Every beyt advances story or sharpens scene.
- **Forms**: مثنوی حماسی (Shahnameh).
- **Common vazn**: فعولن فعولن فعولن فعل (متقارب مثمن محذوف). The Shahnameh is essentially in this single meter.
- **Voice**: epic, restrained, grave; weight without melodrama.
- **Signature moves**:
  - Direct, vivid simile: "چو خورشید بر کوه" — short, image-first.
  - Action verbs in clear sequence: pulled, struck, fled, fell.
  - Heroic epithet: "پهلوانِ یل"، "گُردِ سپاه".
  - Civic virtue named explicitly: خرد، داد، راستی.
  - Scene → decision → action structure inside 2–4 beyt.
- **Vocabulary leans on**: اسب، نیزه، تیغ، خرد، داد، پهلوان، کوه، دشت.
- **Don't**: ornamental language, mystical allusion, romantic introspection, archaisms beyond what natural به Shahnameh diction allows.
- **Common failure**: counterfeit antiquity — using کهن-sounding words on a modern syntactic frame. Ferdowsi's antiquity is structural, not lexical.

## خیام (Khayyam) — compressed paradox

- **Signature**: ضربه‌ی مصراع چهارم — fourth-line landing. Image, question, paradox, and a final line that is both surprising and inevitable.
- **Forms**: رباعی, almost exclusively.
- **Common vazn**: اوزان رباعی (variations on هزج).
- **Voice**: philosophical, terse, sceptical, present-tense.
- **Signature moves**:
  - Concrete image opens (cup, jug, wheel of fortune, wine, dust, today).
  - A question or doubt enters in beyt 2 / مصراع 2.
  - Paradox in مصراع 3.
  - Fourth مصراع *lands* — it neither summarises nor moralises; it pivots.
- **Vocabulary leans on**: کوزه، باده، خاک، چرخ، گل، لحظه، دی، فردا، امروز.
- **Don't**: pad with adjectives, moralise the fourth line, stretch into rubaiyat-like sequences (Khayyam is single-rubai, not narrative).
- **Common failure**: a fourth مصراع that explains the third instead of pivoting. If you can predict the fourth line from the first three, scrap and rewrite.

## فارابی-وار (Farabi-ish) — philosophical clarity with image

- **Signature**: گزاره‌ی روشن + صورت خیال‌انگیز — clear thought wrapped in a single sharp image.
- **Forms**: قطعه، رباعی، short بحث-style poems.
- **Common vazn**: any well-scanning meter; choose for clarity not effect.
- **Voice**: didactic without preaching; aimed at تربیت (cultivation), not entertainment.
- **Signature moves**:
  - State the proposition once, plainly.
  - Find a concrete object that *enacts* the proposition (lamp behind a wall, mirror without light).
  - Compress to 2 beyts or 1 رباعی; do not extend.
- **Vocabulary leans on**: عقل، خیال، چراغ، آینه، گواه، نشان.
- **Don't**: become a sermon. Don't lose the image to the argument.
- **Common failure**: reads like منظوم philosophy lecture. The test: can a reader who doesn't share the philosophy still feel something from the image?

## شعر نو / ترانه‌ی مدرن (Modern lyric)

- **Signature**: زبانِ گفتن > زبانِ نوشتن — the spoken voice leads. Short lines, repeatable hook, looser meter, tighter emotion.
- **Forms**: ترانه (verse / pre-chorus / chorus / bridge), شعر نیمایی (broken hemistich), شعر سپید (free verse).
- **Common vazn**: free pulse; classical scansion optional. Stress and line length matter most.
- **Voice**: present-tense, intimate, contemporary diction with occasional archaic anchors.
- **Signature moves**:
  - Conversational diction: میخوام، میدونم، نمیشه.
  - Sustained vowel words for chorus (بیا، هنوز، چراغ).
  - Imagery from urban life and modern objects (پنجره، چراغ، تقویم، خیابون).
  - Repetition as structure, not ornament.
- **Don't**: write classical syntax in modern register; the seam shows.
- **Common failure**: prose with line breaks. Modern Persian lyric still demands rhythm — the looser the meter, the tighter the diction must be.
